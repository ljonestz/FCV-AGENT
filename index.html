WBG FCV Analytics Unit

FCV Project Screener

A tool for screening WBG projects for recommendations to strengthen FCV sensitivity and responsiveness

What this tool does

This tool uses an AI language model (LLM) to screen draft WBG project documents — such as PCNs and PADs — for FCV dynamics. It generates structured recommendations on how to strengthen FCV sensitivity and responsiveness, drawing on the WBG FCV Strategy Refresh, the OST Manual on FCV Sensitivity and Responsiveness, the FCV Operational Playbook, and Good Practice Notes on Peace & Inclusion Lenses and FCV-Sensitive Programming — alongside the uploaded documents and the AI's broader knowledge of the country and sector context.

The tool is designed to support — not replace — expert FCV analysis. It works best as a starting point for peer review conversations between FCV specialists and Task Team Leaders.

The tool automatically detects the type of project document uploaded — PCN, PID, PAD, Additional Financing, Restructuring Paper, or ISR — and adjusts the depth, framing, and tone of its analysis accordingly. A concept note triggers broader, more exploratory research; an ISR produces operationally-focused supervision priorities.

How to use it — three stages
Stage 1 · Context & Extraction

Upload your project document — PCN, PID, PAD, Additional Financing, Restructuring Paper, or ISR. The tool auto-detects the document type, runs automated web research on the country context, and extracts all FCV-relevant content. Review what it finds and correct anything before proceeding.

Stage 2 · FCV Assessment

The AI assesses the project's FCV sensitivity and responsiveness using the full OST framework — 12 recommendations, 25 key questions, and a Do No Harm checklist. Detailed analytical evidence is available in expandable "Under the Hood" panels.

Stage 3 · Recommendations Note

A collegial peer-review memo addressed to the TTL with strategic priority cards tailored to the project's lifecycle stage. Each priority includes specific actions, PAD language, and implementation guidance. Optional "Go Deeper" panels provide alternative approaches and Playbook references.

Refining the analysis

After each stage, the AI will ask you 1–2 clarifying questions about things it is uncertain about. Answering these improves the quality of subsequent stages. You can also use the Refine box at any point to correct the AI, add local knowledge, or redirect its focus. The AI's reasoning is visible throughout — you are in control of the analysis.

Important — use with care
  • Outputs are generated by an AI and may contain errors, omissions, or misjudgements. They should not be treated as definitive expert analysis.
  • The AI draws on the WBG FCV Strategy Refresh, OST Manual, FCV Playbook, and Good Practice Notes on Peace & Inclusion Lenses and FCV-Sensitive Programming, supplemented by its general knowledge of the country context, which may be outdated or incomplete. Always verify against current, authoritative sources.
  • The Recommendations Note should be reviewed, adapted, and validated by an FCV specialist before any operational use.
  • Do not upload classified, confidential, or otherwise restricted documents. Only use materials cleared for use with external AI processing systems.
