WBG FCV Analytics Unit

FCV Sensitivity Project Screener

A tool for screening WBG projects for recommendations to strengthen FCV sensitivity and responsiveness

What this tool does

This tool uses an AI language model (LLM) to screen draft WBG project documents — such as PCNs and PADs — for FCV dynamics. It generates structured recommendations on how to strengthen FCV sensitivity and responsiveness, drawing on the uploaded documents, the WBG FCV Sensitivity & Responsiveness Guide, and the AI's broader knowledge of the country and sector context.

The tool is designed to support — not replace — expert FCV analysis. It works best as a starting point for peer review conversations between FCV specialists and Task Team Leaders.

How to use it — four stages
Stage 1 · Identifying FCV Risks

Upload your PCN or PAD. The AI extracts all FCV-relevant content from the document and flags information from its own knowledge of the country context. Review what it finds and correct anything that is wrong or missing before proceeding.

Stage 2 · FCV-Sensitivity Screening

The AI assesses the project across six FCV dimensions, rating risks both to the project (how the FCV context threatens success) and from the project (how it could inadvertently worsen fragility). A risk matrix summarises the findings.

Stage 3 · Identifying Project Gaps

The AI identifies gaps in FCV sensitivity and design, proposes mitigations for high-risk dimensions, and runs a do-no-harm checklist. This content feeds directly into the final Recommendations Note — review it carefully before proceeding.

Stage 4 · Recommendations Note

A collegial peer-review memo addressed to the TTL. It summarises FCV strengths and gaps, sets out strategic priorities, and includes a technical annex. The output includes a mandatory AI disclaimer and can be copied directly into Word.

Refining the analysis

After each stage, the AI will ask you 1–2 clarifying questions about things it is uncertain about. Answering these improves the quality of subsequent stages. You can also use the Refine box at any point to correct the AI, add local knowledge, or redirect its focus. The AI's reasoning is visible throughout — you are in control of the analysis.

Important — use with care
  • Outputs are generated by an AI and may contain errors, omissions, or misjudgements. They should not be treated as definitive expert analysis.
  • The AI draws on general knowledge of the country context, which may be outdated or incomplete. Always verify against current, authoritative sources.
  • The Recommendations Note should be reviewed, adapted, and validated by an FCV specialist before any operational use.
  • Do not upload classified, confidential, or otherwise restricted documents. Only use materials cleared for use with external AI processing systems.
