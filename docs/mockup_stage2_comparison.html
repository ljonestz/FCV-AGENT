Synthesis

FCV Sensitivity: The project demonstrates partial awareness of Honduras' FCV context but falls short on specificity — criminal violence dynamics, indigenous rights, and security protocols are underaddressed. Design adaptations exist but are generic rather than calibrated to the criminal-violence context type.
FCV Responsiveness: The project does not actively engage with root causes of fragility. Economic connectivity is framed as a development outcome, not as a pathway to address gang-driven fragility. No FCV operational flexibilities are leveraged. Responsiveness is the project's weakest dimension.

FCV Assessment: Honduras Sustainable Connectivity Project

FCV Sensitivity Assessment

Is the project aware of and designed for the FCV context?

Contextual Awareness Anticipate Differentiate

The project identifies Honduras' structural fragilities and recurring crisis cycles. However, criminal violence analysis remains superficial — "illicit activities" without naming MS-13 or Barrio 18 territorial control in Cortés. No RRA reference despite HIGH-risk context.

Design Adaptation Differentiate

Implementation arrangements acknowledge SIT's limited WB experience. Geographic targeting uses poverty data. But design is not calibrated to criminal-violence context — standard government execution without alternative modalities.

Targeting & Inclusion Differentiate

Poverty-based geographic focus is appropriate but doesn't disaggregate by conflict exposure. Blanket assertion of no Indigenous Peoples contradicts documented Lenca populations. Limited analysis of crime-affected communities as distinct beneficiary group.

Do No Harm

8 Principles: 3 addressed 4 partial 1 gap
GBV specialists planned but no comprehensive security protocols for 1,500-worker influx in criminal-violence corridors. Grievance mechanism planned but accessibility for marginalised groups undetailed. No conflict-sensitive impact monitoring.

M&E & Safeguards Anticipate

No FCV-specific indicators, conflict-sensitive monitoring, or early-warning systems. Standard E&S monitoring insufficient for transport project in gang-affected territory.

FCV Responsiveness Assessment

Does the project actively work to change the FCV situation?

Root-Cause Engagement Jobs & PS

Connectivity framed as a development outcome but not linked to addressing economic drivers of gang recruitment or youth migration. No explicit jobs or livelihoods pathway. The project misses an opportunity to connect infrastructure to economic resilience in one of Central America's most violence-affected corridors.

Operational Flexibilities Enhanced Toolkit

No CERC, GEMS, TPM, HEIS, or alternative implementation arrangements utilised. No remote supervision tools despite security constraints. The absence of FCV operational flexibilities in a HIGH-risk context is the project's most significant responsiveness gap.

Institutional Strengthening Differentiate

Limited connection between project M&E and broader state-citizen relationship building. Grievance mechanism is procedural rather than designed to strengthen accountability. No citizen engagement beyond standard consultations.

Regional & Displacement Dimensions Anticipate

Despite connecting to the Guatemala trade corridor and Honduras' 247,000 IDPs, no regional spillover analysis or displacement-sensitive design. Cross-border dynamics unaddressed.
