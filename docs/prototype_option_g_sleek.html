How it works

Upload a document.
Walk away with a clear set of recommendations.

The screener reads your project document, researches the country context, checks it against WBG standards, and delivers a set of specific, actionable recommendations — without any further input from you. Here is what happens at each step.

Stage 1 of 3
1
Read your document and research the country

You upload your project document — a PCN, PID, PAD, or similar. The screener reads every section carefully. At the same time, it runs a set of live web searches to build a picture of what is happening right now in the project country: conflict conditions, fragility trends, key risks. You can see exactly where every piece of information came from.

Stage 2 of 3
2
Check your project against WBG standards

The screener checks your project against four sets of WBG guidance — the OST Manual, the FCV Strategy Refresh, the FCV Playbook, and Good Practice Notes on peace and inclusion. It works through dozens of standards and questions. It also makes a distinction that matters: is the project sensitive to the FCV context (aware of it, designed around it), or is it responsive to it (actively working to change it)? Both are assessed and rated separately.

46
standards and questions checked
12 OST standards · 25 diagnostic questions · 9 Do No Harm principles
Do No Harm comes first. A 9-point checklist asks whether the project could inadvertently make things worse — through how resources flow, who is included, or what risks go unaddressed.
Sensitivity vs responsiveness — a critical distinction. A project can be conflict-aware without actively addressing root causes. The screener rates both separately and explains the difference.
Nothing hidden. Every standard checked, every question asked — all visible in expandable panels so you can see exactly what was looked for and what was found.
From this assessment, the most important gaps are identified and turned into specific recommendations.
then
Stage 3 of 3
3
Get recommendations for your specific document

The output is a memo written for the Task Team Leader — clear, specific, and directly tied to the document you uploaded. Every recommendation names a particular section: the PDO, the results framework, the environmental commitments. It says what to add or change, and suggests the exact wording. It also tells you when to act.
