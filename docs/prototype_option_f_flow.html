How it works

Upload a document. Walk away with a clear set of recommendations.

The screener takes your project document and — without any further input — reads it, researches the country context, checks it against WBG standards, and tells you exactly what needs strengthening and where. Here is what happens at each stage.

1
Stage 1 Read your document and research the country

You upload your project document — a PCN, PID, PAD, or similar. The screener reads every section of it carefully. At the same time, it runs a set of web searches to find out what is happening right now in the project country: conflict conditions, fragility trends, key risks. You get to see exactly where every piece of information comes from.

Three layers of evidence. Your uploaded documents carry the most weight. Live web research adds current context. The AI's own knowledge fills gaps — and is always labelled separately.
Country classification built in. The screener places the project country within the WBG's four FCV categories and uses this to shape everything that follows.
With a full picture of your document and country context in hand, the analysis begins.

2
Stage 2 Check your project against WBG standards

The screener checks your project against four sets of WBG guidance — the OST Manual, the FCV Strategy Refresh, the FCV Playbook, and Good Practice Notes on peace and inclusion. It works through dozens of standards and questions. It also makes a distinction that matters: is the project sensitive to the FCV context (aware of it and designed around it), or is it responsive to it (actively trying to change it)? These are assessed and rated separately.

46
standards and questions checked 12 OST standards · 25 diagnostic questions · 9 Do No Harm principles
Do No Harm comes first. A 9-point checklist asks whether the project could inadvertently make things worse — through how resources flow, who is included, or what risks go unaddressed.
Sensitivity vs responsiveness — a critical distinction. A project can be conflict-aware without actively addressing root causes. The screener rates both separately and explains the difference.
Nothing is hidden. All the working — every standard checked, every question asked — is visible in expandable "Under the Hood" panels.
From this assessment, the most important gaps are identified and turned into specific recommendations.

Stage 3 Get recommendations for your specific document

The output is a memo written for the Task Team Leader — clear, specific, and directly tied to the document you uploaded. Every recommendation points to a particular section: the PDO, the results framework, the environmental commitments. It says what to add or change, and even suggests the exact wording. It also tells you when to act.

Adapted to the country context. Recommendations reflect the specific FCV category of the project country — what works in a post-conflict state looks different from what works in a country at fragility risk.
